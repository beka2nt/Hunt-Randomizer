Weapons

From Hunt: Showdown 1896 Wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Weapons are the main way to deal damage in Hunt: Showdown 1896. All base weapons are available to buy for Hunt Dollars and can be used from Bloodline Rank 0, but their variants (such as attached scopes, bayonets and silencers) have to be unlocked through gaining Weapon XP with that specific weapon family before they can be bought.

A lot of weapons also have legendary skins, which can be obtained in various ways. All available skins and their respective source are listed on the weapons pages.

In Bounty Hunt and Bounty Clash, players can equip a Hunter with their desired loadout of Weapons, Tools and Consumables before the match, then spawn in with them.
In Soul Survivor, players have to search the map for Weapons to equip and fight with.

Weapon Types

Weapons in the game are categorized in three different ways: Weapon Slot Size, Ammo Type and Action Type.

Weapon Slots

Each Hunter is able to equip two weapons in their loadout, which can be any combination of weapons that fit the available space. Weapons range from 1-3 'slots':

  • Large Slot : Full-length Rifles and Shotguns, as well as Special weapons such as the Crossbow or Bomb Lance.
  • Medium Slot : Swan-off Rifles and Shotguns, Pistols equipped with stocks, Dual-Wielded pistol pairs and Special weapons such as the Hunting Bow or Bomb Launcher.
  • Small Slot : Pistols, Melee Weapons and Special weapons such as Hand Crossbow.

Hunters are able to carry up to 4 slots worth of weapons, where a large slot and a small slot weapon or two medium slot weapons are usual combinations.

With the Trait Quartermaster the Hunter can carry 5 slots, allowing him to combine a large slot with a medium slot. During some Events, the Trait Gunrunner even allows for 6 slots making it possible two carry two large weapons.

Ammo Size

There are five main types of ammunition:

  • Compact Ammo: Small caliber weapons with lower damage, muzzle velocity and penetration, but a smoother bullet drop over distance and the largest ammo pool (20-28 extra for rifles and 12-21 for pistols)
  • Medium Ammo: Medium caliber weapons with higher damage, muzzle velocity and penetration, with slightly steeper bullet drop and less ammo than Compact (12-24 extra for rifles and 12 for pistols)
  • Long Ammo: Large caliber weapons with the highest damage, muzzle velocity and penetration, but with steep bullet drop and the least ammo (10-18 extra for rifles and 6-14 for pistols)
  • Shotgun Shells: 12 & 20 gauge shotgun shells that fire a spread of 14 pellets over short distance
  • Special Ammo: Non-traditional ammunition that follows its own rules, such as Crossbows, Hunting Bows, Bomb Launchers, the Nitro Express and the Dolch 96

The general behavior of each ammo type can be tweaked with Custom Ammo to increase penetration or muzzle velocity or create status effects such as bleeding, burning or poisoning. More information on the various ammo types can be found on the Ammo page.

Action Type

To further differentiate weapons aside from size and ammo, subcategories exist which reflect the kinds of stats each weapon has. These categories aren't represented by official designations, but how the weapon performs.

  • Bolt Action: Rifles that pull a bolt back to cycle a new cartridge into the chamber.
    • Benefits from the Iron Eye trait to keep sights on target while working the bolt.
    • Some can benefit from the Bulletgrubber trait to save ejected live rounds when reloading.
  • Lever Action: Rifles and shotguns that cock a lever to cycle a new cartridge.
    • Benefits from the Iron Eye trait to keep sights on target while working the lever.
    • Especially benefits from the Levering trait to hipfire the weapon very quickly
  • Pump Action: Shotguns and rifles that pump a sliding handguard to cycle a new cartridge.
    • Can benefit from the Iron Eye trait, though it does little for shotguns which are equally accurate in hipfire and ADS
    • Some can benefit from the Bulletgrubber trait to save ejected rounds when reloading
  • Single-Shot: Rifles, shotguns, pistols, crossbows and bomb launchers that manually load a single cartridge or bolt into the chamber.
    • Rifles benefit from the Fast Fingers trait to reload the weapon faster from a handful of bullets kept in the supporting hand
    • Single-Shot weapons can split their ammo pool between two ammo types, such as regular and custom, or two types of custom ammo.
  • Double-Barrel: Rifles and shotguns that can be fired twice before needing to be manually reloaded
  • Semi-Automatic and Automatic: Rifles, shotguns and pistols that load a new round automatically after each trigger pull, or when the trigger is held down
    • Far more expensive than any other kind of weapon
  • Single-Action: Revolvers that require the hammer to be manually cocked back before firing
    • Benefits from the Fanning trait to hipfire the weapon very quickly
  • Double-Action: Revolvers that automatically cock the hammer back when pulling the trigger
    • Cannot benefit from the Fanning trait

Weapon Statistics

Weapon Stats of the Conversion Chain Pistol

Weapon Stats determine the strength and weaknesses of each respective weapon, and can be modified by Weapon Variants and Custom Ammo.

Damage

  • Damage value of a shot that hits a Hunter in the upper torso at 10 meters
  • For shotguns, this is the average damage at 10 meters at center mass

Drop Range

  • Distance (in meters) at which a projectile drops roughly a head's height (20cm) below the aim point
  • Displayed in the HUD when Aiming Down Sights
  • Rate of drop varies by ammo type and is modified by velocity, barrel length and weapon action types

Rate of Fire

  • Shots fired per minute, including time to reload

Cycle Time

  • The time (in seconds) a weapon needs to be ready for the next shot, including time to reload for single-shot weapons
  • For Dual Wield: the time it takes for the weapon that shot first to be ready again

Spread

  • The relative crosshair spread of a weapon while aiming from the hip
  • Shotguns have a comparatively wider spread

Sway

  • The relative amount of weapon sway while ADS

Vertical Recoil

  • The strength of the vertical recoil (in degrees) after a shot

Reload Speed

  • The time (in seconds) it takes to fully reload the weapon when empty
  • Includes any special behavior such as clip reloading or cycling the weapon before the last round can be inserted

Muzzle Velocity

  • The velocity (meters per second) at which a weapon's projectile travels
  • Projectiles travel in a ballistic arc

Melee Damage

  • Damage value of a light or heavy melee attack that hits a Hunter in the upper torso

Stamina Consumption

  • The stamina consumed (out of 100) with a light or heavy melee attack

Outdated Stats

These stats are no longer present to view, as they were replaced by other statistics.

Effective Range

  • The range (in meters) that weapons will kill a Hunter in one hit – usually a headshot
  • Replaced in Update 2.0 with Drop Range, as headshots became lethal at any range

Handling

  • An abstract percentage rating of effectiveness taking into account; weapon sway, recoil and bullet spread
  • Replaced in Update 1.12 with Spread, Sway and Vertical Recoil statistics to give more granular information
